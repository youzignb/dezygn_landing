# The AI Creative Client System — VSL Script (v1)

**Format:** on-page hero VSL · **Target length:** ~4 minutes (≈640 spoken words @ ~160 wpm)
**Framework:** Ryan Deiss 12-beat VSL, compressed for a $37 tripwire, + an explicit *unique-mechanism* beat (RMBC) — which we already own: **Proof Before Pitch**.
**Voice:** Bertrand — first person, contractions, calm and plain. No hype. Every number is real (see facts lock at bottom).
**Delivery:** OLD-SCHOOL SLIDE VSL — **no on-camera presenter.** Animated typographic slides (big on-screen text, key words emphasized, simple reveals) over a voiceover, intercut with the real screenshots + client photos. Captions burned in, word-synced (sound-off safe). Voice: Kokoro TTS scratch now to lock timing → swap in Bertrand's real voiceover later.
**Three-CTA escalation:** Desire → Logic → Fear (Deiss signature).

> Column key — **[ON-SCREEN]** = what the viewer sees (B-roll / screenshot / text card). **VO** = spoken line. Timecodes are targets, not locks.

---

### BEAT 1 — OPEN · Pattern interrupt + proof (0:00–0:18)
*Deiss open type used: **Proof**. Hook must land in the first 3 seconds — 50–60% of drop-off happens there.*

**[ON-SCREEN]** Hard cut, no logo: the real Upwork conversation screenshot fills frame, "40+ applicants" badge highlighted. Word-synced caption pops the key words.

**VO:**
"Forty freelancers applied for this job. Thirty-nine sent a message. I sent something else — and I won it.
Not because I was cheaper. Not because I had a wall of five-star reviews. I didn't.
I won because of one small move almost nobody makes. Once you see it, you can't unsee it."

### BEAT 2 — PROBLEM + PROMISE (0:18–0:50)
*Name the exact problem, promise the resolution, plant the open loop.*

**[ON-SCREEN]** B-roll: an Upwork job feed scrolling; three identical proposal bubbles stack on screen — "I can do this." / "I'm an expert." / "Check my portfolio."

**VO:**
"Here's the deal. Every day, ecommerce brands post jobs for AI product photos. Lifestyle shots. Ad creatives. They need them constantly.
And every day, the same forty freelancers reply with the same three lines. Promise. Promise. Promise.
Now picture the client. She's about to wire money to a stranger on the internet. She's not excited — she's nervous. Forty people are telling her what they *could* do, and she has no idea who's real.
Stick with me for ninety seconds and I'll show you exactly how I make her pick me — before I've said a word about myself."

### BEAT 4 — AGITATE (1:00–1:30)
*Twist the knife: the cost of staying in the begging pile.*

**[ON-SCREEN]** Montage: proposals marked "viewed — no reply," a race-to-the-bottom price ticker dropping, a freelancer refreshing an empty inbox.

**VO:**
"If you've pitched on Upwork or Fiverr, you know this feeling. You write the perfect proposal. You drop your price. You wait. Viewed… no reply.
You're not losing because you're bad. You're losing because you're invisible — one more promise in a stack of forty. And every week you stay invisible, someone with half your talent wins the job because they did one thing differently."

### BEAT 5 — THE REVEAL · Unique mechanism (1:30–2:05)
*The relief. Name the mechanism. This is the RMBC "unique mechanism" beat.*

**[ON-SCREEN]** Bertrand to camera. Then the magic moment: a raw phone snapshot of a product (the real lotion bottle) → wipe → the finished client-ready lifestyle shot. Big serif card: **"Proof Before Pitch."**

**VO:**
"So here's what I do instead. Before I apply… I make the image.
Her actual product. Her brand's style. Done with AI in a few minutes — and I attach it to my proposal.
Now there's nothing to compare. Everyone else said they could. I already did. I call it Proof Before Pitch — and right now it pays me fourteen hundred and eighty dollars a month. Every month. From two clients."

## Production notes
- **Length:** keep the cut at **3.5–4.5 min** for the on-page VSL. Also cut a **60–90s ad version** (Beats 1, 4, 5, 7, 12) for paid social.
- **Hook:** first 3 seconds = the won-job screenshot + "Forty freelancers applied… I won." Nothing before it.
- **Captions:** burned-in, word-synced (sound-off safe) — generate via `hyperframes transcribe` after the voiceover.
- **Proof cadence:** never hold a static talking head >~5s; cut to B-roll/screenshots on every claim.
- **The timer/price-step shown in Beats 3/11 must be genuinely honored** (price actually rises). Jaded buyers will test it.
