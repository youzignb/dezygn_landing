The Visual Syntax Cheatsheet.

Every client-ready AI photo is engineered from the same six ingredients. Stop gambling with prompts — control the ingredients, control the outcome.

The 3 Rules
Rule 1

Every image has 6 ingredients

Style, Subject, Action, Scene, Camera, Brand. Control these, control the outcome.

Rule 2

Each ingredient = words or pictures

Use reference images for accuracy. Use text where you want the model to interpret.

Rule 3

Order matters — first is strongest

The model pays the most attention to what comes first. Lead with what matters most.

The 6 Ingredients
IngredientThe questionWhat to define
1STYLEWhat kind of photo is this?Editorial, UGC, catalog, luxury, vintage, paparazzi…
2SUBJECTWhat or who is the hero?Product details, model demographics, key features

Multimodal Anchoring · words vs pictures
→

Product accuracy: anchor with a reference image [image1] — never describe a product you can show.

→

Specific pose: use a pose reference photo instead of describing limbs and angles.

→

Style consistency across a set: reuse one style anchor image for the whole batch.

→

Golden rule: don't over-describe what your images already show. Text fights references.

Before you generate
1

Write the six ingredients down before opening the tool. Most failures happen before generation.

2

Decide words vs pictures for each ingredient (Rule 2).

3

Put the ingredient that matters most for this shot first (Rule 3).

4

Generate, then QA against the source: materials, texture, colors, proportions, scale.
